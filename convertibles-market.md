<p><ul><li>Vanilla Convertible Bond</li><li>Mandatory Convertible Bond</li><li>Reversible Convertible Bond</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The convertible bond market includes several types primarily defined by their conversion features. A vanilla convertible bond allows holders to convert bonds into a predetermined amount of stock at specified times. A mandatory convertible bond requires automatic conversion to equity at maturity, typically designed for less risk-averse investors. A reversible convertible bond offers the option to convert back into bonds at a later date, providing flexibility for investors navigating market fluctuations. Each type caters to different investor preferences and risk profiles.</p></p>
