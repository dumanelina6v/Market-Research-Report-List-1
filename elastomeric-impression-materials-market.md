<p><p>Elastomeric impression materials are crucial in dental applications, available in several types. Addition silicone is known for its accuracy and stability, making it popular for detailed impressions. Condensation silicone is less expensive but may have dimensional stability issues. Polyether offers excellent detail and moisture resistance, while polysulfide is favored for its flexibility and tear resistance, though it has a longer setting time. Other materials include hydrocolloids and vinyl siloxane, each catering to specific clinical needs and preferences.</p></p>
